Electric Motorcycle Battery & BMS Design

2024 - Present Surge Electric Motorcycle Team Project In Progress

Overview

Currently designing and building a high-performance lithium-ion battery pack and battery management system (BMS) for an electric motorcycle project. The system utilizes 224 Samsung 21700 cells arranged in a modular 28S8P configuration to deliver reliable power, thermal management, and safety features for electric vehicle applications.

Battery Pack Specifications

  • Cell Type: Samsung 21700 lithium-ion cells (high energy density, ~5000mAh)
  • Module Configuration: 7S8P (7 series, 8 parallel) = 56 cells per module
  • Total System: 4 modules in series → 28S8P configuration (224 total cells)
  • Nominal Voltage: ~100.8V (3.6V × 28S)
  • Total Capacity: ~40Ah (5000mAh × 8P)
  • Energy Storage: ~4 kWh total system capacity

Battery Management System (BMS)

  • Cell Balancing: Implementing active balancing circuitry to equalize cell voltages
  • Voltage Monitoring: Real-time tracking of individual cell voltages to prevent over/under-voltage conditions
  • Current Sensing: Precision current measurement for State of Charge (SOC) estimation
  • Thermal Management: Temperature sensors with thermal shutdown protection
  • Safety Features: Over-current protection, short-circuit detection, and emergency shutdown
  • Communication: CAN bus interface for vehicle integration and diagnostics

Mechanical & Electrical Design

  • Modular Architecture: Four independent battery boxes for easy maintenance and thermal isolation
  • Busbar Design: Custom copper busbars for low-resistance series connections between modules
  • Wiring Harness: High-gauge silicone wire for parallel connections and BMS integration
  • Enclosure: Vibration-resistant housing with IP65 protection rating
  • Bill of Materials: Complete BOM including cells, BMS components, connectors, and hardware
  • Thermal Interface: Thermal pads and heat sinks for effective heat dissipation

Technologies & Skills

Lithium-Ion Battery Design Battery Management Systems Power Electronics CAD (OnShape) Electrical Engineering Thermal Analysis CAN Bus Protocol Safety Standards (UL/IEC)

Design Challenges

This project involves balancing multiple engineering constraints including energy density, thermal management, safety compliance, cost optimization, and packaging within the motorcycle's limited space. Key challenges include designing robust cell-to-cell connections, implementing reliable BMS algorithms for accurate SOC estimation, and ensuring the system can handle high discharge currents during acceleration while maintaining safe operating temperatures.

What I'm Learning

This hands-on project is providing deep experience in battery chemistry, power electronics, embedded systems programming, and mechanical integration. I'm gaining practical knowledge in electrical safety standards, thermal modeling, manufacturing processes for battery assembly, and the real-world trade-offs involved in electric vehicle design. Working with a team has also enhanced my collaboration and project management skills.
