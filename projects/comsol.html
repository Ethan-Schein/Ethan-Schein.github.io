Overview

I developed COMSOL Multiphysics models to evaluate and compare the diffusion and delivery profiles of two model drugs— hydrocortisone and caffeine—through human skin using solid and hollow microneedle geometries. The goal was to determine which microneedle design delivers drugs more efficiently to the dermis layer and eventually into the bloodstream.

By analyzing the transport dynamics of these two molecules with different physicochemical properties, I aimed to predict the expected behavior of doxorubicin (DOX), a chemotherapy drug with a significantly larger molecular weight (~543 g/mol) than either test compound.

Project Goal: Quantitatively compare solid vs hollow microneedle delivery efficiency using hydrocortisone (362.46 g/mol) and caffeine (194.19 g/mol) as model compounds, then extrapolate findings to predict doxorubicin delivery behavior.

COMSOL Modeling Setup

Model Geometries

I constructed two distinct microneedle configurations to compare passive (solid) versus active (hollow) delivery mechanisms:

Solid Microneedle Configuration
  • Needle length: 500 μm
  • Outer diameter: Tapered geometry
  • Drug coating: Uniform layer on needle surface
  • Transport mechanism: Diffusion-only (no convection)
  • Péclet number: Pe = 0 (pure diffusion)
Hollow Microneedle Configuration
  • Needle length: 500 μm
  • Lumen diameter: Internal hollow bore
  • Flow rate: 2.976 × 10⁻⁴ m/s
  • Transport mechanism: Combined convection and diffusion
  • Péclet numbers: Pe = 68.9 (hydrocortisone), Pe = 35.2 (caffeine)

Material Properties & Parameters

I used literature-derived data to calculate diffusion coefficients and partition coefficients (through COMSOL) for each solute. All other variables were kept constant between models to ensure fair comparison:

Key Material Parameters
  • Hydrocortisone:
    • Molecular weight: 362.46 g/mol
    • Diffusion coefficient: 2.16 × 10⁻⁹ cm²/s
    • Partition coefficient: Higher affinity for lipid layers
  • Caffeine:
    • Molecular weight: 194.19 g/mol
    • Diffusion coefficient: 4.23 × 10⁻⁹ cm²/s
    • Partition coefficient: Lower due to higher polarity

Constant Parameters Across Models

  • Needle length: 500 μm
  • Lumen diameter (hollow only)
  • Needle outer diameter
  • Epidermis thickness
  • Dermis thickness
  • Epidermis density
  • Dermis density
  • Flow rate (hollow only): 2.976 × 10⁻⁴ m/s

Transport Mechanisms

I calculated Péclet numbers to understand the dominant transport mechanism for each configuration:

Péclet Number Analysis: Using Pe = vL/D, where v is velocity, L is characteristic length, and D is diffusion coefficient.

Hydrocortisone (hollow): Pe = (2.976×10⁻⁴ m/s × 500 μm) / (2.16×10⁻⁹ cm²/s) = 68.9
Caffeine (hollow): Pe = (2.976×10⁻⁴ m/s × 500 μm) / (4.23×10⁻⁹ cm²/s) = 35.2

Since Pe ≫ 1, convection dominates over diffusion in the hollow microneedle lumen. In contrast, solid microneedles have Pe = 0, indicating diffusion is the sole transport mechanism.

Results

Hydrocortisone Delivery

I simulated hydrocortisone diffusion through human skin for both microneedle geometries. The solid microneedle model demonstrated significantly deeper and faster penetration compared to the hollow microneedle model.

Key observation: Solid microneedles enabled hydrocortisone to reach the dermis layer efficiently, while hollow microneedles resulted in drug remaining localized near the microneedle insertion site with a much slower delivery rate into the bloodstream.

Caffeine Delivery

I followed the same modeling process for caffeine, using different partition and diffusion coefficient values while keeping all other parameters constant.

Efficiency Ratio Analysis

I calculated efficiency ratios by comparing the time required for each microneedle type to reach a toxic threshold concentration. The efficiency ratio quantifies how much faster one design delivers drug compared to the other.

Caffeine Efficiency Ratio

Ratio: 2.0

Solid microneedle was 2× faster than hollow microneedle to reach toxic threshold concentration

Hydrocortisone Efficiency Ratio

Ratio: 2.0

Solid microneedle was 2× faster than hollow microneedle to reach toxic threshold concentration

Finding: The efficiency ratios calculated for caffeine and hydrocortisone were both found to be 2. This means that the solid microneedle was 2× faster than the hollow microneedle to reach the toxic threshold concentration for both solutes.

Discussion

Molecular Property Effects on Diffusion

The solutes' varying diffusion times can be explained by a number of characteristics including partition coefficients, polarity, and molecular size.

Caffeine vs Hydrocortisone

  • Molecular weight: Caffeine (194.19 g/mol) is substantially smaller than hydrocortisone (362.46 g/mol), which favors a higher diffusion coefficient for caffeine.
  • Partition coefficient: Caffeine's higher polarity causes a lower partition coefficient across the skin barrier, diminishing its capacity to efficiently penetrate the layers of skin.
  • Initial skin entry: Despite caffeine's higher diffusion coefficient due to its smaller size, its lower partition coefficient decreases its initial skin entry.
  • Partitioning vs diffusivity: Hydrocortisone reaches effective concentrations faster due to its stronger partitioning into lipid layers, proving that depending on the transport context, partitioning can sometimes be more important than diffusivity.

Implications for Doxorubicin Delivery

These results provide insight into the expected behavior of doxorubicin (DOX), which has a molecular weight of approximately 543 g/mol—significantly higher than both hydrocortisone and caffeine.

DOX Prediction: Since larger molecules generally exhibit lower diffusion coefficients and slower transdermal transport, it is reasonable to infer that DOX would display even slower penetration kinetics than either test compound. The 2× efficiency advantage of solid microneedles observed for caffeine and hydrocortisone likely applies to DOX as well, though the absolute delivery rates would be lower.

Transport Mechanism Validation

The Péclet number calculations validated my assumptions about the dominant transport mechanisms in each geometry:

  • Hollow microneedle: Pe values of 68.9 (hydrocortisone) and 35.2 (caffeine) indicate that convection dominates over diffusion in the lumen. This justifies using convection-diffusion physics.
  • Solid microneedle: Pe = 0 confirms diffusion is the sole transport mechanism, validating the diffusion-only physics implementation.

Conclusions

Key Findings

  1. Solid microneedles are 2× more efficient: Both caffeine and hydrocortisone showed efficiency ratios of 2.0, meaning solid microneedles delivered drugs to toxic threshold concentrations twice as fast as hollow microneedles.
  2. Deeper and faster penetration with solid design: Solid microneedles enabled drugs to reach the dermis layer efficiently, while hollow microneedles kept drugs more localized near the insertion site.
  3. Partitioning can dominate over diffusivity: Hydrocortisone reached effective concentrations faster than caffeine despite having a lower diffusion coefficient, demonstrating that partition coefficients (lipophilicity) can be more important than molecular diffusivity for skin penetration.
  4. Transport mechanism validation: Péclet number analysis confirmed that convection dominates in hollow microneedles (Pe ≫ 1) while pure diffusion drives solid microneedle delivery (Pe = 0).
  5. DOX prediction: Given doxorubicin's larger molecular weight (~543 g/mol), it would likely exhibit even slower transdermal transport than either test compound, though the 2× efficiency advantage of solid microneedles should still apply.
